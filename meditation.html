About Meditation

Training Attention, Settling the Mind

Dhyana — sustained, effortless awareness — is the seventh limb of classical yoga, arising once concentration deepens and settles. Rather than "emptying the mind," meditation trains attention itself, gradually loosening its grip on distraction and reactivity.

Mantra · Breath · Awareness

Ajapa Japa

Literally "repetition without repeating," Ajapa Japa links the mantra So-Ham to each inhale and exhale until awareness of the breath continues on its own, without conscious effort.

Ajapa Japa · Mantra & Breath
01

Settle & Observe

Sit comfortably, eyes closed, simply observing the natural breath without changing it.

02

Link the Mantra

Mentally repeat "So" on the inhale and "Ham" on the exhale, in rhythm with the breath.

03

Follow the Flow

Attention gently follows the breath's path, undistracted by passing thought.

04

Effortless Awareness

With practice, the awareness continues on its own — repetition without repeating.
