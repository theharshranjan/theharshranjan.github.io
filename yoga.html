Patanjali's Yoga Sutras

Ashtanga — The Eight Limbs

अष्टाङ्ग · The complete path to liberation

Codified by Maharshi Patanjali in the Yoga Sutras (~400 BCE), Ashtanga Yoga is not merely a physical practice — it is a complete eight-fold path that systematically dissolves the fluctuations of the mind and leads the practitioner to Samadhi.

1
First Limb

Yama

यम · Social Ethics

  • ·Ahimsa — Non-violence
  • ·Satya — Truthfulness
  • ·Asteya — Non-stealing
  • ·Brahmacharya — Celibacy / Energy conservation
  • ·Aparigraha — Non-greed
2
Second Limb

Niyama

नियम · Personal Observances

  • ·Shaucha — Purity
  • ·Santosha — Contentment
  • ·Tapas — Austerity
  • ·Svadhyaya — Self-study
  • ·Ishvara Pranidhana — Surrender to the Divine
3
Third Limb

Asana

आसन · Posture

A steady and comfortable posture that prepares the body and nervous system for deeper practice. We teach classical Hatha asanas with correct alignment, breath integration, and awareness.

Key Asanas Taught:

Surya Namaskar · Trikonasana · Sarvangasana · Sirsasana · Paschimottanasana · Padmasana

4
Fourth Limb

Pranayama

प्राणायाम · Breath Extension

Extension and regulation of prana (life force) through breath. The bridge between the outer and inner limbs — calming the nervous system and preparing the mind for meditation.

Techniques:

Nadi Shodhana · Kapalabhati · Bhramari · Ujjayi · Sheetali · Bhastrika

5
Fifth Limb

Pratyahara

प्रत्याहार · Withdrawal

Withdrawal of the senses from their objects. The turning of attention inward — a bridge between the outer practices (Bahiranga) and the inner practices (Antaranga) of yoga.

Practice:

Yoga Nidra · Trataka · Shanmukhi Mudra · Inner Silence Practices

6
Sixth Limb

Dharana

धारणा · Concentration

Single-pointed concentration on an object — a flame, a mantra, a chakra, or the breath. Training the mind to remain unwaveringly focused on one point.

Objects of Focus:

Ajna Chakra · Anahata · Mantra Repetition · Bindu · Trataka (candle gazing)

7
Seventh Limb

Dhyana

ध्यान · Meditation

Unbroken flow of attention toward the object of concentration. The meditator, the act of meditating, and the object of meditation begin to merge. The observer dissolves into observation.

Forms:

Ajapa Japa · Vipassana · Soham Dhyana · Chakra Dhyana · Antar Mouna

8
Eighth Limb

Samadhi

समाधि · Absorption

Complete absorption — the highest state of yoga where individual consciousness merges with universal consciousness. The goal of all spiritual practice.

"Yogaś citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ" — Yoga is the cessation of the fluctuations of the mind.

— Patanjali, Yoga Sutras I.2

The Journey

Bahiranga → Antaranga → Samyama

Yama → Niyama → Asana → Pranayama → Pratyahara → Dharana → Dhyana → Samadhi ✦
The Breath Sciences

Pranayama Techniques

प्राणायाम · Control of Prana

Prana is the life force that animates every cell. Through pranayama, we learn to regulate this energy — calming the nervous system, purifying the nadis (energy channels), and preparing the mind for meditation.

Balancing

Nadi Shodhana

Alternate Nostril Breathing

Purifies the 72,000 nadis (energy channels). Balances left and right hemispheres of the brain. Reduces anxiety, harmonises the nervous system, and brings profound mental clarity.

Dosha

All types

Best For

Anxiety · Sleep · Focus

Energising

Kapalabhati

Skull-Shining Breath

Rapid rhythmic exhalations that cleanse the frontal lobes, activate the digestive fire, expel stale carbon dioxide, and energise the entire system. A Shatkarma (cleansing technique) and pranayama combined.

Dosha

Kapha · Vata

Best For

Digestion · Energy · Clarity

Calming

Bhramari

Humming Bee Breath

The vibration of humming creates a powerful internal resonance that instantly calms the mind, reduces cortisol, relieves migraines, and prepares the practitioner for deep meditation.

Dosha

Pitta · Vata

Best For

Stress · Insomnia · Anger

Warming

Ujjayi

Ocean / Victorious Breath

A gentle constriction of the throat creates a soft oceanic sound with every breath. Used during asana practice to maintain focus, regulate internal heat, and synchronise movement with breath.

Dosha

Vata · Kapha

Best For

Asana · Focus · Warmth

Cooling

Sheetali / Sheetkari

Cooling Breath

Inhalation through a rolled tongue (or teeth) cools the entire system, reduces Pitta, calms inflammation, lowers blood pressure, and brings a sense of serenity and detachment.

Dosha

Pitta · Summer

Best For

Anger · Heat · BP

Purifying

Bhastrika

Bellows Breath

Rapid forceful inhalations and exhalations like a blacksmith's bellows. Ignites the Agni (digestive fire), purifies the blood, energises every cell, and rapidly clears blockages in the pranic body.

Dosha

Kapha · Vata

Best For

Lethargy · Immunity · Fire

Solar

Surya Bhedana

Right Nostril Breathing

Activates Pingala nadi (solar channel), heating the body, stimulating the sympathetic nervous system, increasing vitality and alertness. Used to overcome depression and lethargy.

Dosha

Kapha · Vata

Best For

Depression · Fatigue · Warming

Lunar

Chandra Bhedana

Left Nostril Breathing

Activates Ida nadi (lunar channel), cooling the body, activating the parasympathetic nervous system, promoting rest, creativity, and introspection. Ideal for evening practice.

Dosha

Pitta · Summer

Best For

Evening · Cooling · Creativity

Advanced

Kumbhaka

Breath Retention

The art of breath suspension — Antara Kumbhaka (after inhale) and Bahya Kumbhaka (after exhale). When mastered under a teacher, Kumbhaka awakens the Kundalini and leads directly to higher states of consciousness.

"Between the inhale and exhale lives eternity."

"Kriya Yoga is the scientific method of God-union."

— Paramahansa Yogananda

Purification Through Action

Kriya Yoga

क्रिया योग · The Yoga of Action

Kriya Yoga is both a preparation for deep meditation and a complete path in itself. Described in Patanjali's Yoga Sutras as the practical yoga of daily life, it burns away the impurities of the mind and sense-organs through three sacred disciplines — purifying the being at every level.

त

Tapas — Austerity & Discipline

The deliberate practice of self-discipline — physical austerity, fasting, cold bathing, silence (Mauna), and consistent daily sadhana. Tapas generates heat that purifies the nadis, burns karmic imprints (samskaras), and develops willpower and endurance.

Mauna (Silence) Upavasa (Fasting) Japa
स्व

Svadhyaya — Self-Study

The systematic study of sacred texts (Vedas, Upanishads, Bhagavad Gita) and, more crucially, deep introspective observation of one's own mind, patterns, conditioning, and desires. Mantra repetition is also a form of Svadhyaya.

Scripture Study Mantra Japa Journaling
ई

Ishvara Pranidhana — Surrender

Complete surrender of the fruits of all actions to the Divine. The dissolution of the ego's claim over results — acting with full effort but releasing attachment to outcomes. The highest spiritual act that rapidly accelerates liberation.

Puja Bhakti Nishkama Karma

Classical

Hatha Yoga

Ha · Sun + Tha · Moon

The balancing of solar (Pingala) and lunar (Ida) energies through physical postures, pranayama, mudras, and bandhas. The Hatha Yoga Pradipika (15th century) is its foundational text. Every style of modern yoga — Iyengar, Vinyasa, Yin — flows from Hatha.

Asanas Taught

84 classical postures · Surya Namaskar · Inversions · Twists · Backbends · Forward folds

Also Includes

Shatkarmas (6 cleansing practices) · Mudras · Bandhas · Trataka · Nauli

The Serpent Power

Kundalini Yoga

The awakening of the dormant Kundalini Shakti at the base of the spine, guiding it through the Sushumna nadi and the seven chakras to the Sahasrara at the crown. A profound and carefully guided journey that requires preparation and a teacher.

Chakra Activation — Muladhara to Sahasrara
Kriya Sequences — Specific sets for each chakra
Mantras & Mudras — Bija mantras, Shambhavi
Safe, Supervised Awakening — Never forced

Psychic Sleep

Yoga Nidra

The Art of Conscious Sleep

Yoga Nidra is a state of consciousness between waking and sleeping — where the body is completely relaxed but the mind remains aware. One hour of Yoga Nidra equals four hours of sleep. It works with Sankalpa (intention) to reprogram the subconscious mind.

The Yoga of Sound

Nada Yoga

The path of using sound — both external (Ahata) and internal (Anahata) — as the vehicle for meditation. Sound vibrations calm the mind, activate specific chakras, and ultimately dissolve into the primordial silence from which all creation arises.

Ahata Nada — External Sound

Vocal mantra, singing bowls, bells, tanpura, Kirtan — sound produced by physical instruments

Anahata Nada — Inner Sound

The unstruck sound heard in deep meditation — the primordial OM that reverberates without physical cause

Before Asana & Pranayama

Shatkarmas

षट्कर्म · Six Purification Practices

The Hatha Yoga Pradipika describes six powerful cleansing kriyas that purify the body and pranic channels before deeper practices. These prepare the gross body for the subtlety of pranayama and meditation.

💧

Neti

Nasal Cleansing

Jala Neti (saline water) and Sutra Neti (rubber catheter) cleanse the nasal passages, sinuses, and Ajna chakra. Relieves rhinitis, sinusitis, and improves clarity.

🌀

Dhauti

Digestive Cleansing

Cleansing of the entire alimentary canal — Kunjal Kriya (stomach washing), Vastra Dhauti (cloth swallowing), and Shankha Prakshalana (intestinal cleanse). Eliminates kapha and purifies Agni.

🔥

Nauli

Abdominal Churning

Isolation and rotation of the rectus abdominis muscles, massaging all abdominal organs. Stimulates digestive fire, improves metabolism and gut health. The crown jewel of Hatha Yoga.

✨

Trataka

Fixed Gazing

Unblinking gaze at a candle flame or black dot. Purifies the eyes, strengthens Ajna chakra, develops telepathic powers, and is the most powerful preparation for meditation.

💨

Kapalabhati

Frontal Brain Cleanse

Rapid rhythmic exhalations cleanse the frontal lobes of carbon dioxide, stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, and activate the brain. Both a Shatkarma and pranayama technique.

⚡

Basti

Colon Cleansing

Yoga Basti — drawing water into the rectum through a bamboo tube while in Utkatasana (deep squat). Removes accumulated Apana, balances Vata dosha, and purifies the lower body.

Seals & Locks

Mudras & Bandhas

Mudras

Gesture · Seal · Attitude

Mudras are sacred gestures of the hands, body, and face that direct the flow of prana and create specific states of consciousness. There are over 100 mudras in classical yoga.

Jnana Mudra

Knowledge seal — tip of index to thumb. Stimulates the brain, reduces anxiety.

Chin Mudra

Consciousness seal — palms upward. Invites receptivity and inner awareness.

Shambhavi Mudra

Eyebrow centre gaze — activates Ajna chakra, induces Pratyahara.

Khechari Mudra

Tongue rolled back to soft palate. Stimulates Bindu Visarga, activates nectar.

Bandhas

Energy Locks

Bandhas are muscular contractions that lock prana within specific regions of the body, intensifying its concentration and directing it upward through the Sushumna nadi.

Mula Bandha

Root lock — contraction of the perineum. Activates Muladhara chakra, awakens Kundalini, and retains Apana prana.

Uddiyana Bandha

Abdominal lock — drawing the abdomen up and in after full exhalation. Activates Manipura chakra, massages abdominal organs, and stimulates the solar plexus.

Jalandhara Bandha

Throat lock — chin pressed to chest during Kumbhaka. Activates Vishuddhi chakra, prevents prana from escaping upward and Soma from dripping down.

Maha Bandha

The Great Lock — all three bandhas applied simultaneously during Kumbhaka. The most powerful pranayama practice, producing a powerful surge of prana through Sushumna.
