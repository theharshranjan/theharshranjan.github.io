The Origin

A Journey Inward
That Became a Calling

Harsh Ranjan's path to healing did not begin in a classroom. It began at the age of twenty-two, during a period of profound personal crisis — chronic illness, anxiety, and a searching that modern medicine could not satisfy. It was in the ancient texts of Ayurveda and the practice halls of Yoga that he first encountered the possibility of true wholeness.

Drawn to Rishikesh, he trained under classical Ayurvedacharyas and Yoga masters, spending years in immersive study of Hatha Yoga, Pranayama, Mantra Shastra, and the philosophical traditions of Advaita Vedanta. He simultaneously pursued formal training in depth psychology and somatic therapy, recognising that no single tradition held the complete map of the human mind.

This integration — the ancient and the modern, the physical and the psychological, the scientific and the sacred — became the foundation of Samarpan. The word itself means "surrender": the willingness to offer oneself fully to the process of healing.

"Healing is not what you do to a person. It is what you create space for them to do to themselves."

— Harsh Ranjan

The Path

A Practitioner's Journey

2012 — Rishikesh

First Immersion in Yoga & Vedanta

Left a corporate career to undertake a year-long immersion at a traditional Yoga Ashram. Studied classical Hatha Yoga, Pranayama, and the Upanishads under gurukul guidance.

2014 — Varanasi

Ayurveda Deepening & Mantra Shastra

Studied classical Ayurveda under senior Ayurvedacharyas. Simultaneously initiated into Mantra Shastra and Tantra traditions. Began working with clients for the first time.

2016 — New Delhi

Training in Depth Psychology

Formal training in Jungian psychology, somatic therapy, and trauma integration. Recognised the essential need to bridge Eastern wisdom and Western psychological science.

2018 — Founding of Samarpan

The Offering Begins

Samarpan was born — a sanctuary for integrative healing combining Ayurveda, Yoga, Psychology and Mantra. First international clients from the UK and UAE within the first year.

2020 — Sacred Yatra Programme

Pilgrimages as Healing

Launched the Sacred Yatra programme — guided pilgrimages to Rishikesh, Varanasi, Kedarnath and beyond. A unique offering combining outer journey with inner transformation.

2026 — Present

500+ Lives, 6 Countries & Growing

Today Samarpan serves a global community of seekers through online sessions, retreats, and the Inner Circle — a monthly wisdom newsletter reaching over 2,400 subscribers.
