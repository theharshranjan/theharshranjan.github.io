Ayurvedic Food Science

The Six Tastes (Shadrasa)

Ayurveda recognises six tastes — each with a unique effect on the body and mind. A balanced meal includes all six.

🍯

Madhura (Sweet)

Nourishes tissues, calms Vata & Pitta, promotes strength. Found in rice, milk, ghee, and dates.

🍋

Amla (Sour)

Stimulates digestion, kindles Agni, grounds Vata. Found in lemon, tamarind, yoghurt, and amla.

🧂

Lavana (Salty)

Hydrates tissues, calms Vata, improves appetite. Found in rock salt, seaweed, and mineral-rich foods.

🌶️

Katu (Pungent)

Burns toxins, stimulates metabolism, reduces Kapha. Found in ginger, chilli, garlic, and onion.

🌿

Tikta (Bitter)

Detoxifies blood, cools Pitta, reduces Kapha. Found in fenugreek, bitter gourd, and turmeric.

🫐

Kashaya (Astringent)

Tightens tissues, reduces Pitta & Kapha. Found in legumes, pomegranate, raw banana, and tea.

Ritucharya

Eating with the Seasons

Ayurveda recognises six seasons (Ritucharya), each requiring a distinct dietary approach to maintain perfect health.

Vasanta (Spring)

Kapha Reduction

Light, dry, and bitter foods. Avoid heavy dairy and sweets. Honey, barley, and astringent greens. Ideal for detox protocols.

Grishma (Summer)

Pitta Cooling

Cooling, sweet, liquid-rich foods. Coconut water, cucumber, sweet fruits, rice, and ghee. Avoid excess spice and fermented foods.

Varsha (Monsoon)

Agni Kindling

Warm, digestive-spiced foods. Avoid raw and heavy foods. Ginger tea, light soups, old grains, rock salt, and honey-based preparations.

Sharada (Autumn)

Pitta Pacification

Sweet, light, and bitter foods. Avoid sour and pungent. Fresh pomegranate, snake gourd, rice, lentils, and ghee preparations.

Hemanta (Early Winter)

Vata Nourishment

Unctuous, sweet, and nourishing foods. Ghee, sesame, wheat, meat broth, warm milk with spices, and root vegetables.

Shishira (Late Winter)

Deep Nourishment

Heavy, nourishing, and strengthening foods. Maximise Ojas-building nutrients — Chyawanprash, urad dal, dairy, and warming spiced dishes.
