The Science of Balance

"In every body dwells a unique balance.
Ayurveda is the art of restoring it."

Ayurveda, meaning 'science of life,' is not merely a system of treating disease — it is a comprehensive philosophy of living in harmony with nature. Your body is governed by three doshas (Vata, Pitta, Kapha), each representing different elements and functions. When these doshas are in balance, you experience optimal health, energy, and clarity. Imbalance leads to disease.

At Samarpan, we conduct a detailed assessment of your unique constitution (Prakriti) and current imbalance (Vikriti), then design a personalised protocol combining diet, lifestyle, herbal remedies, and therapeutic practices to restore equilibrium at the deepest level.

Ancient Constitution

The Three Doshas

Every being is a unique combination of elements and energies.

🌬️

Vata

Air & Ether

Governs movement, circulation, creativity, and communication. Vata personalities are energetic, imaginative, and quick-thinking.

Imbalanced Vata May Cause:

  • • Anxiety and insomnia
  • • Dry skin and constipation
  • • Joint pain and weakness
  • • Restlessness and scattered mind

Balancing Practices:

  • • Warm, grounding foods
  • • Oil massage (Abhyanga)
  • • Meditative yoga
  • • Calming herbs (Ashwagandha)
🔥

Pitta

Fire & Water

Governs metabolism, digestion, transformation, and intelligence. Pitta personalities are sharp, ambitious, and driven.

Imbalanced Pitta May Cause:

  • • Inflammation and acne
  • • Acid reflux and ulcers
  • • Anger and irritability
  • • Premature greying or hair loss

Balancing Practices:

  • • Cooling, sattvic foods
  • • Moon-bathing and calm
  • • Cooling breathwork
  • • Liver-supporting herbs
🌊

Kapha

Earth & Water

Governs structure, lubrication, stability, and compassion. Kapha personalities are grounded, nurturing, and loyal.

Imbalanced Kapha May Cause:

  • • Weight gain and sluggishness
  • • Congestion and colds
  • • Depression and attachment
  • • Water retention and bloating

Balancing Practices:

  • • Vigorous, warming exercise
  • • Light, spiced foods
  • • Energising pranayama
  • • Detox herbs (Triphala)
