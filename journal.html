A Samarpan Signature

Two Traditions, Read Side by Side

Select articles carry this dual-column format — classical knowledge on the left, modern evidence on the right — so tradition and research can be read in conversation, not in isolation.

CLASSICAL AYURVEDIC KNOWLEDGE

"ऋतुचर्यायाः पालनं आरोग्यस्य मूलम्।"

Classical texts describe Ritucharya — seasonal regimen — as foundational to preventing disease, aligning diet, sleep and activity with the qualities of each season rather than treating illness after it appears.

MODERN SCIENTIFIC EVIDENCE

Chronobiology research on circadian and circannual rhythms shows measurable seasonal shifts in metabolism, immune markers and sleep architecture — offering a modern vocabulary for what Ritucharya describes.

— Referenced in Issue 03, "Seasons of the Body"
