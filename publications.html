Psychology of the
Sleepless Sleep

Bridging Ancient Yoga Nidra and Modern Neuroscience

An exploration of Yoga Nidra — the science of psychic sleep — as transmitted through the Bihar School of Yoga tradition, placed in conversation with contemporary neuroscientific understanding of sleep, consciousness, and the hypnagogic state. A rare synthesis of lived practice and scientific inquiry.

Discover why the ancients called this state "sleepless sleep" — and what modern sleep science is only now beginning to confirm.

Yoga Nidra Neuroscience Consciousness Bihar School of Yoga
Publication 02

De-Conditioning
the Mind

Where Ancient Tantra Meets Modern Neuroscience

A profound inquiry into the nature of mental conditioning — the grooves of thought, belief, and reactivity that imprison consciousness. Drawing on the tantric understanding of the mind's architecture (Chitta, Manas, Ahamkara, Buddhi) and parallel discoveries in neuroplasticity, this work offers a radical path to freedom.

Ancient Tantra did not seek to suppress the mind — it sought to understand and transcend its deepest patterns. This text is a guide to that liberation.

Publication 03

Cultivating Physical &
Neurochemical Sovereignty
Through the Gut

An Integrative Synthesis of Ayurvedic Wisdom & Cutting-Edge Neuropsychology

The gut is not merely a digestive organ — it is a second brain, a neurochemical factory, a seat of Agni (the sacred digestive fire of Ayurveda). This integrative work explores how gut health governs neurochemistry, mood, immunity, and consciousness through both the lens of Ayurvedic Agni theory and contemporary gut-brain axis research.

True sovereignty — physical, mental, and neurochemical — begins in the belly. This text is the map to reclaiming it.
