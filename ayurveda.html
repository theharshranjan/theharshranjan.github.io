About Ayurveda

A Science of Life, Not Just Medicine

Ayurveda — "the science of life" — is a system of medicine over five thousand years old, rooted in the idea that health is a dynamic balance between body, mind and environment. Rather than treating symptoms in isolation, it looks at each person's unique constitution to guide diet, lifestyle and treatment.

Classical Preparation · Herbal Formulations

Panchamahabhuta

The five elements — earth, water, fire, air, ether — that form all matter, including the body.

Tridosha

Vata, Pitta and Kapha — the three biological energies that govern every physical and mental process.

Dhatus

The seven bodily tissues — from plasma to reproductive tissue — nourished in sequence.

Agni

Digestive fire — the metabolic capacity believed to underlie most disease when imbalanced.

Ama

Undigested metabolic residue — a classical concept linked to toxin build-up and disease.

Ojas

The subtle essence of vitality and immunity, cultivated through balanced living.

Prakriti

Your natural, unchanging constitution — set at conception.

Vikriti

Your current state of imbalance — what treatment aims to correct.

Dosha Center

What Are Doshas?

Doshas are the three biological energies — Vata, Pitta and Kapha — that every person carries in a unique proportion. Understanding yours is the starting point for personalised Ayurvedic care.

Tridosha · Vata · Pitta · Kapha

Vata

Air + Ether

Governs movement — circulation, breath, nerve impulses. In balance: creative and quick. Out of balance: anxious, dry, restless.

Pitta

Fire + Water

Governs transformation — digestion, metabolism, intellect. In balance: sharp and driven. Out of balance: irritable, inflamed.

Kapha

Earth + Water

Governs structure — tissue, immunity, stability. In balance: calm and grounded. Out of balance: sluggish, congested.

Integrative, Not a Replacement

Ayurveda can complement conventional care, but it is not a substitute for it. Please continue any prescribed medical treatment and consult your physician before starting Ayurvedic therapies — especially during pregnancy, for children, or alongside existing medication.
