The Definition of Health

Ayurveda defines health (*Swasthya*) not just as the absence of disease, but as a state where the Doshas, digestive fire (*Agni*), tissues (*Dhatus*), and waste products (*Malas*) are balanced, and the Soul, senses, and mind are in a state of bliss.

Vata (Air + Ether): Governs all movement (neural impulses, circulation, breathing). Imbalance leads to anxiety, dryness, and pain.

Pitta (Fire + Water): Governs metabolism (digestion, enzymes, hormones). Imbalance leads to inflammation, anger, and acidity.

Kapha (Water + Earth): Governs structure (lubrication, immunity, stability). Imbalance leads to lethargy, congestion, and weight gain.

Aushadhi: Nature's Intelligence

Herbs are not just chemicals; they are packages of biological intelligence. In Ayurveda Biology, we study the *Pharmacodynamics* of herbs not just by their active alkaloids, but by their *Rasa* (Taste), *Guna* (Quality), *Virya* (Potency), and *Vipaka* (Post-digestive effect).

  • Turmeric (Haridra): Bitter/Pungent. Heating. Scrapes toxins (Lekhana) and reduces inflammation.
  • Ashwagandha: Sweet/Bitter. Heating. Builds tissue (Brimhana) and stabilizes the mind.
  • Brahmi: Bitter. Cooling. Enhances cognitive function (Prajna) and calms the heart.
