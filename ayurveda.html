The Science of Life

आयुर्वेद

"In every body dwells a unique intelligence. Ayurveda is the art of listening to it — and restoring its ancient harmony."

Ayurveda, meaning 'science of life,' is not merely a system of treating disease — it is a comprehensive philosophy of living in harmony with nature. Your body is governed by three doshas (Vata, Pitta, Kapha), each representing different elements and functions.

At Samarpan, we conduct a detailed assessment of your unique constitution (Prakriti) and current imbalance (Vikriti), then design a personalised protocol combining diet, lifestyle, herbal remedies, and therapeutic practices to restore equilibrium at the deepest level.

Your Constitution

The Three Doshas

Every being is a unique song of elemental energies.

वात

Vata Dosha

Air · Ether · Movement

Vata governs all movement — every nerve impulse, breath, and circulation. Vata types are creative, quick, and adaptable, but prone to anxiety and irregularity when out of balance.

When Imbalanced

Anxiety & insomnia
Dry skin, constipation
Joint pain & weakness
Scattered, restless mind

To Balance

Warm, grounding foods
Oil massage (Abhyanga)
Meditative yoga
Ashwagandha & Shatavari
Balance Your Vata →
पित्त

Pitta Dosha

Fire · Water · Transformation

Pitta governs digestion, metabolism, and intelligence. Pitta types are sharp, courageous, and ambitious — but when inflamed, become irritable, perfectionistic, and prone to inflammation.

When Imbalanced

Inflammation & acne
Acid reflux & ulcers
Anger & irritability
Hair loss & greying

To Balance

Cooling, sattvic foods
Moon-bathing & calm
Sitali & Chandra pranayama
Amalaki & Bhringraj
Balance Your Pitta →
कफ

Kapha Dosha

Earth · Water · Structure

Kapha governs structure, stability, and compassion. Kapha types are grounded, nurturing, and deeply loyal — but when stagnant, accumulate weight, congestion, and emotional heaviness.

When Imbalanced

Weight gain & sluggishness
Congestion & colds
Depression & attachment
Water retention, bloating

To Balance

Vigorous, warming exercise
Light, spiced foods
Kapalabhati pranayama
Triphala & Guggulu
