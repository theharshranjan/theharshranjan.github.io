Our Modalities

Eight evidence-based and wisdom-rooted approaches — chosen for you, not applied uniformly.

01 · Somatic Experiencing

Healing Through the Body

Developed by Dr Peter Levine, Somatic Experiencing (SE) is a body-centred approach to healing trauma and stress disorders. It works by gently tracking physical sensations, completing interrupted survival responses held in the nervous system, and restoring the body's innate capacity to self-regulate.

Unlike traditional therapy, SE doesn't require you to re-tell or re-live your trauma story. Instead, we follow the body's wisdom — noticing where life force is held, where it is blocked, and guiding its natural release.

Best For

Trauma, PTSD, chronic stress, freeze states

Session Length

60–75 minutes per session

"The body is the unconscious mind."

— Candace Pert

02 · Depth Psychology

Meeting the Shadow

Rooted in the traditions of Jung, Hillman, and Adler, depth psychology explores the unconscious — the vast, largely uncharted territory that drives most of our behaviour, choices, and suffering. Shadow work, dream analysis, and archetypal exploration reveal what we have hidden even from ourselves.

At Samarpan, we weave Jungian depth work with Vedantic self-inquiry — making it uniquely suited to those seeking both psychological and spiritual understanding of their inner landscape.

Best For

Identity crises, repetitive patterns, spiritual growth

Depth

Long-term, transformative work

"Until you make the unconscious conscious, it will direct your life."

— Carl Jung

03 · CBT & Schema Therapy

Rewiring the Mind

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy identifies the automatic thoughts, core beliefs, and behavioural cycles that maintain anxiety, depression, and self-sabotage. Schema Therapy extends this by reaching the deep emotional patterns formed in childhood — the "schemas" that shape how we relate to ourselves and the world.

These are structured, evidence-based approaches offering concrete tools: thought records, behavioural experiments, schema mapping, and mode work that create measurable, lasting change.

Best For

Anxiety, depression, OCD, self-worth

Approach

Structured, goal-oriented, evidence-based

"You don't have to believe every thought you think."

04 · Trauma Release & EMDR-Informed Work

Unlocking the Freeze

Trauma freezes us in time — the nervous system remains stuck in the past even as the present moves on. Our trauma-informed work draws from Somatic Experiencing, TRE (Tension & Trauma Releasing Exercises), EMDR-informed processing, and Polyvagal Theory to gently complete the survival responses that remain unfinished.

We move at the pace of your nervous system, honouring the body's innate wisdom throughout the process. Healing trauma is not about reliving — it is about resolving.

Best For

Complex trauma, PTSD, freeze, dissociation

Safety

Titrated, slow, and carefully paced

"The body remembers what the mind forgets — and the body also knows how to heal."

05 · Yoga Nidra Therapy

Healing in the Space Between

Yoga Nidra, often called "yogic sleep," guides practitioners to the hypnagogic threshold between waking and sleeping — a state of profound receptivity where deep psychological and neurological reprogramming becomes possible. Unlike sleep, you remain conscious; unlike meditation, the effort is effortless.

At Samarpan, Yoga Nidra is used therapeutically — seeding specific Sankalpa (intentions), releasing emotional residues, processing grief, and addressing depression and chronic fatigue at their energetic roots.

Best For

Depression, insomnia, exhaustion, emotional numbness

Format

Guided 45–60 min lying-down session

"One hour of Yoga Nidra equals four hours of sleep."

— Swami Satyananda

06 · Therapeutic Breathwork

The Most Direct Path

The breath is the only autonomic function we can consciously control — making it a direct bridge between the conscious mind and the unconscious body. Specific breathing techniques profoundly alter nervous system states, brain chemistry, and emotional landscapes.

Our breathwork draws from Pranayama (Nadi Shodhana, Bhramari, Kapalabhati), Holotropic principles, and modern nervous system science — used therapeutically for anxiety resolution, anger processing, grief release, and deep states of clarity.

Best For

Panic, anxiety, anger, grief, emotional blockages

Techniques

Pranayama, holotropic, box breathing

"Breath is the king of mind."

— B.K.S. Iyengar

07 · Vedantic Self-Inquiry

Who Am I?

Rooted in Advaita Vedanta and the teachings of Ramana Maharshi, Vedantic inquiry (Atma Vichara) addresses the deepest root of human suffering — the mistaken identification of the Self with the conditioned mind. When we discover who we truly are beyond the ego, the existential anxiety that underlies all other anxieties begins to dissolve.

This is ideal for clients who have done traditional therapy but feel something deeper remains unresolved — a sense of meaninglessness, existential dread, or spiritual longing that psychology alone hasn't touched.

Best For

Existential crises, identity, spiritual seekers

Tradition

Advaita Vedanta, Non-duality, Jnana Yoga

"Happiness is your nature. It is not wrong to desire it."

— Ramana Maharshi

08 · Transpersonal Psychology

Beyond the Personal Self

Transpersonal psychology, pioneered by Maslow, Grof, and Wilber, integrates spiritual and transcendent dimensions of the human experience. It honours mystical states, peak experiences, spiritual emergencies, and the call of the soul as legitimate and important aspects of psychological health.

At Samarpan this framework is particularly valuable for clients navigating spiritual emergence, kundalini experiences, psychedelic integration, near-death experiences, or a deep longing for contact with something beyond the personal.

Best For

Spiritual emergence, integration, meaning-making

Tradition

Maslow, Grof, Wilber, Eastern traditions

"Psychology needs to acknowledge and study the farther reaches of human nature."

— Abraham Maslow

Your Journey With Us

Every session is a sacred container — held with presence, patience, and deep professional care.

01
First Contact

Free Discovery Call (20 min)

We begin with a no-obligation discovery call where you share what brings you to seek support. We assess fit, explain our approach, and answer your questions. There is no pressure and complete confidentiality from the very first moment.

02
Assessment

Intake & Deep Assessment (60 min)

The first full session is a comprehensive intake covering your history, symptoms, core wounds, relationship patterns, spiritual background, and goals. From this, a tailored therapeutic roadmap is created — choosing the modalities most suited to your unique constitution and presenting concerns.

03
Active Work

Weekly Sessions & Integration

Regular weekly or fortnightly sessions using your chosen modalities. Each session is held in a trauma-sensitive container with space for depth, silence, and authentic emergence. Between sessions, you receive integration tools — practices, journalling prompts, and somatic exercises.

04
Consolidation

Review, Transition & Continued Growth

At key milestones we review progress, celebrate transformation, and decide the path forward — whether to deepen the work, transition to monthly maintenance, or gracefully complete the therapeutic relationship. Many clients choose to continue as part of Samarpan's community.
