Practical Daily Tips

Simple, actionable habits for everyday wellness

Simple Meal Framework

Balance is key for sustainable nutrition

  • ½ vegetables, ¼ whole grains, ¼ protein
  • Include legumes for protein & fiber
  • Smart snacking: nuts, fruit, yogurt

Daily Habits

Small changes that make a big difference

  • Start with water & warm lemon/ginger
  • Eat mindfully without screens
  • Include raw greens or salad daily

Student & Budget Tips

Healthy eating on a budget

  • Batch cook: daal, curries, proteins
  • Use seasonal vegetables (cheaper!)
  • Frozen vegetables reduce waste

Hydration & Beverages

Supporting wellness through smart choices

  • Prefer plain water over sweetened drinks
  • Tea/coffee OK — minimize added sugar
  • Get electrolytes from food, not drinks

Nutrition Myths Debunked

Separating fact from fiction with evidence

Carbs make you fat

Fact: Excess calories cause weight gain, not carbs themselves. Focus on quality: prefer whole grains and avoid refined, sugar-dense carbs.

Fat is always bad

Fact: Healthy fats (nuts, seeds, vegetable oils) are essential. Limit saturated & trans fats as advised by WHO.

Supplements replace food

Fact: Whole foods provide fiber, phytochemicals, and balanced nutrients. Always follow professional guidance.

Detox diets are necessary

Fact: Your liver and kidneys detoxify daily. Support them with hydration, fiber, and balanced meals — restrictive cleanses aren't necessary.

Ayurveda Notes

Practical Ayurvedic pointers focused on digestion

  • Eat warm & cooked: Easier digestion; prefer warm fluids if digestion is weak
  • Digestive spices: Cumin, coriander, fennel, and ginger aid digestion in small amounts
  • Meal timing: Finish main meal when the sun is relatively high; avoid heavy late-night meals
  • Dosha-aware tweaks: (Vata) warm soups; (Pitta) cooling foods; (Kapha) lighter meals and more greens

These notes are educational and tailored during sessions — not a substitute for clinical advice.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about nutrition sessions

Are these personalized medical recommendations?

No. This is educational and coaching. For medical conditions consult your physician or a registered dietitian and share reports during sessions.

Do you follow Indian dietary guidelines?

Yes — core advice aligns with ICMR-NIN Dietary Guidelines for Indians and WHO healthy diet principles.

Can you accommodate vegetarian/vegan diets?

Absolutely! Plans are flexible and can accommodate vegetarian, vegan, and non-vegetarian preferences.

How many sessions will I need?

This varies by individual and goals. Some clients find value in a single session for specific guidance, while others prefer ongoing support. We can discuss what feels right during our first session.
