The Samarpan Approach

Meditation Is Not
Emptying the Mind.

"It is learning to witness the mind without being enslaved by it."

At Samarpan, meditation is not taught as a relaxation technique — it is offered as a profound science of consciousness. Rooted in the Tantric and Vedic traditions, our practices move you from mental noise into the vast silence beneath thought. Whether you are a complete beginner or an advanced practitioner, each session is calibrated to exactly where you are — and where you need to go.

What We Teach

Meditation Practices

Each technique opens a different door into the same vast inner space.

Most Transformative
01

Breath · Mantra · Consciousness

Ajapa Japa

The "unchanted mantra" — synchronising the natural breath cycle with the primal sound So'Ham. Unlike active mantra repetition, Ajapa Japa dissolves the meditator into the rhythm of existence itself. A gateway to Samadhi.

Synchronises breath with primal sound
Dissolves egoic thought patterns
Advanced path to Samadhi states
Begin Practice
02

Sleep · Subconscious · Healing

Yoga Nidra

"Psychic sleep" — a guided journey between waking and deep sleep where the subconscious becomes fully accessible. One hour of Yoga Nidra is said to equal four hours of ordinary sleep. Profoundly healing for trauma, anxiety, and insomnia.

Profound rest at a cellular level
Heals trauma stored in the body
Reprogrammes limiting beliefs
Begin Practice
03

Insight · Impermanence · Freedom

Vipassana

The oldest of Buddha's meditation techniques — observing bodily sensations with equanimity to understand the impermanent, ever-changing nature of all experience. Builds the neural pathways of non-reactivity and inner freedom.

Develops equanimity under pressure
Breaks habitual reaction patterns
Insight into the nature of suffering
Begin Practice
04

Focus · Clarity · Third Eye

Trataka

The Hatha Yoga practice of fixed gazing — traditionally on a candle flame, or an internal point. Trataka purifies the eyes, sharpens concentration to a single point, and activates the Ajna chakra (third eye). Powerful for those who struggle with a wandering mind.

Develops one-pointed concentration
Strengthens mental clarity & memory
Awakens intuitive perception
Begin Practice
05

Sound · Vibration · Inner Silence

Nada Meditation

Meditation on the "inner sound" — tuning into the subtle inner vibration called Anahata Nada. Combined with mantra and singing bowl resonance, this practice leads awareness from gross sound into profound inner silence. Deeply calming for the nervous system.

Calms the nervous system rapidly
Dissolves mental chatter effortlessly
Gateway to deep meditative states
Begin Practice
06

Mantra · Japa · Devotion

Mantra Meditation

Structured repetition of sacred sound using mala beads (Japa) or silent mental repetition. A personalised mantra is selected based on your Prakriti and intention. Over time, the mantra becomes a living force — arising spontaneously, anchoring consciousness in the present.

Personalised mantra selection
Structured daily Japa practice
Builds unwavering inner stillness
Begin Practice

Why Meditate

What Changes

Anxiety & Stress

Consistent practice measurably reduces cortisol, quiets the default mode network, and rewires anxious thought loops at the neural level.

Sleep & Rest

Yoga Nidra and breath-based practices restore deep delta wave sleep, reduce night-time mental agitation, and end the cycle of exhaustion.

Emotional Balance

Witness-consciousness training creates space between stimulus and reaction — so you respond from wisdom rather than old conditioning.

Focus & Clarity

Trataka and Vipassana sharpen attentional control, improve working memory, and build the capacity for deep, uninterrupted concentration.

Spiritual Growth

Beyond well-being — Ajapa Japa and Samadhi practices open genuine experiences of expanded consciousness, presence, and inner freedom.

Physical Health

Reduces blood pressure, balances the autonomic nervous system, improves immune function, and supports digestion through the gut-brain axis.

Relationships

As reactivity decreases and self-awareness grows, relationships naturally deepen — conflicts reduce, empathy expands, love becomes unconditional.

Presence

The highest gift — the capacity to inhabit this moment fully. Not thinking about life, but actually living it with complete awareness and aliveness.

Regular mantra chanting (Japa) involves active mental or vocal repetition of a mantra. Ajapa Japa is different — it is the recognition that the primal mantra So'Ham is already being chanted by the breath with every inhalation and exhalation since birth. The practice is about becoming aware of this existing sound, not creating a new one. It is subtler and leads more directly to Samadhi states.
