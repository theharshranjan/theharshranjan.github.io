Ashtanga
The Eight Limbs

अष्टाङ्ग · The complete path to liberation

Codified by Maharshi Patanjali, Ashtanga Yoga is not merely physical practice — it is a complete eight-fold path that systematically dissolves the fluctuations of the mind and guides the practitioner to Samadhi.

1
First Limb

Yama

यम · Social Ethics

  • ·Ahimsa — Non-violence
  • ·Satya — Truthfulness
  • ·Asteya — Non-stealing
  • ·Brahmacharya — Energy conservation
  • ·Aparigraha — Non-greed
2
Second Limb

Niyama

नियम · Personal Observances

  • ·Shaucha — Purity
  • ·Santosha — Contentment
  • ·Tapas — Austerity
  • ·Svadhyaya — Self-study
  • ·Ishvara Pranidhana — Surrender
3
Third Limb

Asana

आसन · Posture

Steady, comfortable posture that prepares body and nervous system for deeper practice. We teach classical Hatha asanas with correct alignment and breath integration.

Key Asanas:

Surya Namaskar · Trikonasana · Sarvangasana · Sirsasana · Padmasana

4
Fourth Limb

Pranayama

प्राणायाम · Breath Extension

Extension and regulation of prana through breath. The bridge between outer and inner limbs — calming the nervous system, preparing the mind for meditation.

Techniques:

Nadi Shodhana · Kapalabhati · Bhramari · Ujjayi · Kumbhaka

5
Fifth Limb

Pratyahara

प्रत्याहार · Withdrawal

Withdrawal of the senses from their objects — turning attention inward. The bridge between Bahiranga (outer) and Antaranga (inner) practices.

Practice:

Yoga Nidra · Trataka · Shanmukhi Mudra · Antar Mouna

6
Sixth Limb

Dharana

धारणा · Concentration

Single-pointed concentration on one object — a flame, a mantra, a chakra, or the breath. Training the mind to remain unwaveringly focused.

Objects of Focus:

Ajna Chakra · Mantra Repetition · Bindu · Trataka

7
Seventh Limb

Dhyana

ध्यान · Meditation

Unbroken flow of attention toward the object. The meditator, the act, and the object begin to merge. The observer dissolves into observation.

Forms:

Ajapa Japa · Soham Dhyana · Chakra Dhyana · Antar Mouna

8
Eighth Limb

Samadhi

समाधि · Absorption

Complete absorption — individual consciousness merges with universal consciousness. The goal of all spiritual practice.

"Yogaś citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ"

— Patanjali, Yoga Sutras I.2

Bahiranga (Outer) → Antaranga (Inner) → Samyama (Integration)

Yama→ Niyama→ Asana→ Pranayama→ Pratyahara→ Dharana→ Dhyana→ Samadhi ✦
The Breath Sciences

Pranayama
Nine Techniques

प्राणायाम · Control of Prana

Prana is the life force animating every cell. Through pranayama, we regulate this energy — calming the nervous system, purifying the 72,000 nadis, and preparing the mind for meditation.

Balancing

Nadi Shodhana

Alternate Nostril Breathing

Purifies the 72,000 nadis. Balances left and right brain hemispheres. Reduces anxiety, harmonises the nervous system, and brings profound mental clarity.

Dosha

All types

Best For

Anxiety · Sleep · Focus

Energising

Kapalabhati

Skull-Shining Breath

Rapid rhythmic exhalations cleanse the frontal lobes, activate digestive fire, expel stale CO₂, and energise the entire system. A Shatkarma and pranayama combined.

Dosha

Kapha · Vata

Best For

Digestion · Energy · Clarity

Calming

Bhramari

Humming Bee Breath

Internal humming vibration instantly calms the mind, reduces cortisol, relieves migraines, and prepares the practitioner for deep meditation.

Dosha

Pitta · Vata

Best For

Stress · Insomnia · Anger

Warming

Ujjayi

Ocean / Victorious Breath

Gentle throat constriction creates a soft oceanic sound. Used during asana to maintain focus, regulate internal heat, and synchronise movement with breath.

Dosha

Vata · Kapha

Best For

Asana · Focus · Warmth

Cooling

Sheetali / Sheetkari

Cooling Breath

Inhalation through a rolled tongue cools the entire system, reduces Pitta, calms inflammation, lowers blood pressure, and brings serenity.

Dosha

Pitta · Summer

Best For

Anger · Heat · BP

Purifying

Bhastrika

Bellows Breath

Rapid forceful inhalations and exhalations ignite Agni, purify the blood, energise every cell, and rapidly clear blockages in the pranic body.

Dosha

Kapha · Vata

Best For

Lethargy · Immunity · Fire

Solar

Surya Bhedana

Right Nostril Breathing

Activates Pingala nadi (solar channel), heating the body, stimulating the sympathetic nervous system, increasing vitality and alertness. Used to overcome depression.

Dosha

Kapha · Vata

Best For

Depression · Fatigue · Warming

Lunar

Chandra Bhedana

Left Nostril Breathing

Activates Ida nadi (lunar channel), cooling the body, activating the parasympathetic nervous system, promoting rest, creativity, and introspection.

Dosha

Pitta · Summer

Best For

Evening · Cooling · Creativity

Advanced

Kumbhaka

Breath Retention

The art of breath suspension — Antara Kumbhaka (after inhale) and Bahya Kumbhaka (after exhale). When mastered, Kumbhaka awakens the Kundalini and leads to higher states of consciousness.

"Between the inhale and exhale lives eternity."

"Kriya Yoga is the scientific method of God-union."

— Paramahansa Yogananda

Purification Through Action

Kriya Yoga

क्रिया योग · The Yoga of Action

Kriya Yoga is both a preparation for deep meditation and a complete path in itself. It burns away impurities of the mind and sense-organs through three sacred disciplines — purifying the being at every level.

त

Tapas — Austerity & Discipline

Deliberate self-discipline — fasting, silence (Mauna), cold bathing, consistent daily sadhana. Tapas generates heat that purifies the nadis and burns karmic imprints (samskaras).

Mauna (Silence) Upavasa (Fasting) Japa
स्व

Svadhyaya — Self-Study

Study of sacred texts (Vedas, Upanishads, Bhagavad Gita) and deep introspective observation of one's own mind, patterns, conditioning, and desires.

Scripture Study Mantra Japa Journaling
ई

Ishvara Pranidhana — Surrender

Complete surrender of all actions to the Divine. Dissolution of the ego's claim over results — acting with full effort but releasing attachment to outcomes.

Puja Bhakti Nishkama Karma

Before Asana & Pranayama

Shatkarmas
Six Purifications

षट्कर्म · Hatha Yoga Pradipika

Six powerful cleansing kriyas that purify the body and pranic channels before deeper practices, preparing the gross body for the subtlety of pranayama and meditation.

💧

Neti

Nasal Cleansing

Jala Neti (saline water) and Sutra Neti (rubber catheter) cleanse the nasal passages, sinuses, and Ajna chakra. Relieves rhinitis, sinusitis, and improves mental clarity.

🌀

Dhauti

Digestive Cleansing

Cleansing of the entire alimentary canal — Kunjal Kriya, Vastra Dhauti, and Shankha Prakshalana. Eliminates kapha and deeply purifies Agni.

🔥

Nauli

Abdominal Churning

Isolation and rotation of the rectus abdominis — massages all abdominal organs. Stimulates digestive fire and improves metabolism. The crown jewel of Hatha Yoga.

✨

Trataka

Fixed Gazing

Unblinking gaze at a candle flame. Purifies the eyes, strengthens Ajna chakra, develops intuition, and is the most powerful preparation for meditation.

💨

Kapalabhati

Frontal Brain Cleanse

Rapid rhythmic exhalations cleanse the frontal lobes, stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, and activate the brain. Both a Shatkarma and pranayama.

⚡

Basti

Colon Cleansing

Drawing water into the rectum through a bamboo tube in Utkatasana. Removes accumulated Apana, balances Vata dosha, and purifies the lower body completely.

Seals & Locks

Mudras & Bandhas

Mudras

Gesture · Seal · Attitude

Sacred gestures of the hands, body, and face that direct the flow of prana and create specific states of consciousness. Over 100 mudras exist in classical yoga.

Jnana Mudra

Knowledge seal — index to thumb. Stimulates brain, reduces anxiety.

Chin Mudra

Consciousness seal — palms upward. Invites receptivity and awareness.

Shambhavi Mudra

Eyebrow centre gaze — activates Ajna chakra, induces Pratyahara.

Khechari Mudra

Tongue to soft palate — stimulates Bindu Visarga, activates nectar.

Bandhas

Energy Locks

Muscular contractions that lock prana within specific regions, intensifying its concentration and directing it upward through the Sushumna nadi.

Mula Bandha

Root lock — contraction of the perineum. Activates Muladhara chakra, awakens Kundalini, retains Apana prana.

Uddiyana Bandha

Abdominal lock — drawing abdomen up after full exhalation. Activates Manipura chakra, massages abdominal organs.

Jalandhara Bandha

Throat lock — chin to chest during Kumbhaka. Activates Vishuddhi chakra, prevents prana escaping upward.

Maha Bandha

The Great Lock — all three bandhas applied simultaneously. The most powerful pranayama practice, producing a surge of prana through Sushumna.
