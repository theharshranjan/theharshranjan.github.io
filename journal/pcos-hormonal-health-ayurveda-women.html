PCOS Through the Ayurvedic Lens

In Ayurveda, PCOS is understood primarily as a Kapha-Vata imbalance affecting the Artavaha Srotas (reproductive channels). Excess Kapha causes cystic formation through excessive tissue accumulation; disturbed Vata disrupts the hormonal communication network and causes irregular cycles. The modern approach of hormonal suppression through oral contraceptives addresses nothing at the root — and often deepens the underlying constitutional imbalance.

Ayurvedic Protocol

Dietary Correction: Reducing Kapha-aggravating foods — refined dairy, white sugar, cold foods, heavy grains. Increasing bitter and astringent tastes that reduce tissue accumulation. Warm, light, easily digestible foods with warming spices.

Herbal Support:

  • Shatavari (Asparagus racemosus) — The primary Stri Rasayana, balancing estrogen-like activity without hormonal suppression
  • Ashoka — Classical herb for menstrual regulation and uterine health
  • Lodhra — Reduces ovarian cysts and balances FSH/LH ratios
  • Guduchi — Reduces insulin resistance and systemic inflammation

Yoga Therapy: Baddha Konasana, Supta Virasana, Viparita Karani improve pelvic blood flow and reduce cystic formations with consistent practice.
