Samarpan is a research-driven integrative health initiative committed to bridging the timeless wisdom of Ayurveda with the insights of modern science. By bringing together Ayurveda, Yoga, Psychology, Nutrition, Clinical Practice, Research, Education, and Public Health, we work to make holistic, evidence-informed healthcare accessible, meaningful, and sustainable for individuals and communities alike.

Whether you are seeking better health, deeper understanding, professional learning, or research collaboration, Samarpan offers a space where ancient knowledge and scientific inquiry come together in service of human wellbeing. Every journey begins with understanding. Every step moves toward lasting wellbeing.

What Is Your Prakriti?

Answer 10 questions and discover your Ayurvedic body type — Vata, Pitta, or Kapha — with personalised programme recommendations.

Takes only 3 minutes · Completely free

Vata

Air & Space

Governs movement, creativity and the nervous system. In balance it brings energy and flexibility; out of balance it shows up as anxiety, dry skin or irregular digestion.

Pitta

Fire & Water

Governs metabolism, digestion and focus. In balance it brings clarity and drive; out of balance it shows up as irritability, acidity or inflammation.

Kapha

Earth & Water

Governs structure, immunity and stability. In balance it brings calm and endurance; out of balance it shows up as sluggishness, weight gain or congestion.

Ayurveda holds that everyone is a unique mix of all three doshas, usually with one or two more dominant. Your Prakriti — set at birth — shapes the diet, routine and therapies that work best for your body.

Research Corner

Where Evidence Meets Tradition

A weekly look at modern research through an Ayurvedic lens

Nutrition Science

Turmeric & Inflammation

Study

Clinical research continues to explore curcumin's effect on inflammatory markers in the body.

Ayurvedic view

Haridra (turmeric) has long been used to balance Pitta dosha and support Agni, the digestive fire.

Takeaway

Pair turmeric with black pepper and warm milk at night for better absorption at home.

Mind-Body Science

The Gut-Brain Axis

Study

Emerging research links gut microbiome health to mood regulation and stress response.

Ayurvedic view

Ayurveda has always treated Agni and mental clarity as inseparable, centuries before the term "gut-brain axis" existed.

Takeaway

Favour warm, freshly cooked meals and consistent mealtimes to steady both digestion and mood.

Stress Physiology

Yoga & Cortisol

Study

Studies on regular Pranayama practice show measurable reductions in circulating cortisol levels.

Ayurvedic view

Pranayama is described as a direct lever on Prana Vayu, the subtle air governing the nervous system.

Takeaway

Five minutes of slow, even breathing before bed can meaningfully ease the day's accumulated stress.

Endocrinology

Ashwagandha & Cortisol

Study

Randomised trials associate standardised Ashwagandha extract with lower serum cortisol under chronic stress.

Ayurvedic view

Ashwagandha is classed as a Rasayana, a rejuvenative that steadies Vata and restores Ojas.

Takeaway

Best taken consistently over weeks, not as a one-off remedy on a stressful day.

Sleep Science

Melatonin & Dinacharya

Study

Circadian research shows consistent sleep-wake timing improves melatonin rhythm more than duration alone.

Ayurvedic view

Dinacharya prescribes fixed waking and sleeping hours aligned with Kapha, Pitta and Vata times of day.

Takeaway

A fixed wake time does more for sleep quality than chasing an extra hour in bed.

Immunology

Tulsi & Immune Markers

Study

Preliminary studies suggest Tulsi (Holy Basil) supports immune modulation and lowers inflammatory markers.

Ayurvedic view

Tulsi is revered as an adaptogen that clears Kapha and supports respiratory strength.

Takeaway

A daily cup of Tulsi tea is a simple, low-risk addition to a seasonal immunity routine.

Metabolic Health

Fenugreek & Blood Sugar

Study

Clinical trials link fenugreek seed intake with modest improvements in post-meal glucose response.

Ayurvedic view

Methi is traditionally used to kindle Agni while calming excess Pitta and Kapha.

Takeaway

Soaked fenugreek seeds in the morning are an easy, low-cost place to start.

Women's Health

Shatavari & Hormonal Balance

Study

Early research points to Shatavari's phytoestrogenic compounds supporting hormonal and reproductive health.

Ayurvedic view

Shatavari is considered the primary female Rasayana, nourishing Shukra and Artava dhatus.

Takeaway

Often paired with warm milk and taken through the luteal phase for best effect.

Cardiovascular Science

Arjuna & Heart Health

Study

Small clinical studies associate Arjuna bark extract with improved cardiac function markers.

Ayurvedic view

Arjuna has long been used as a Hridya, a herb specifically indicated for the heart.

Takeaway

Best used alongside, never instead of, prescribed cardiac medication and monitoring.

Neuroscience

Brahmi & Cognitive Function

Study

Trials on Bacopa monnieri (Brahmi) report modest gains in memory retention with sustained use.

Ayurvedic view

Brahmi is classified as a Medhya Rasayana, a herb specifically for the mind and intellect.

Takeaway

Effects build gradually — most studies show benefit only after 8-12 weeks of daily use.

Dermatology

Neem & Skin Microbiome

Study

Research documents Neem's antimicrobial action against common skin-flora imbalances.

Ayurvedic view

Neem is classed as Tikta (bitter) and Pitta-pacifying, traditionally used to purify Rakta (blood).

Takeaway

Topical use suits most skin types, but always patch-test before wider application.

Musculoskeletal Health

Guggulu & Joint Inflammation

Study

Trials on Guggulu extracts report reduced joint stiffness and inflammatory markers in early studies.

Ayurvedic view

Guggulu is prized for clearing Ama and Vata accumulation from the joints and channels.

Takeaway

Often combined with warm oil massage (Abhyanga) for compounded relief.

Respiratory Health

Pranayama & Lung Capacity

Study

Studies on regular Pranayama practice show measurable improvements in lung capacity and oxygen efficiency.

Ayurvedic view

Pranayama is described as the direct discipline of Prana, the vital breath governing all bodily function.

Takeaway

Even 10 minutes of daily Anulom Vilom builds capacity noticeably within a few weeks.

Ayurveda is a 5,000-year-old Indian system of medicine that treats the root cause of disease rather than symptoms. Unlike modern medicine's one-size-fits-all approach, Ayurveda recognises that every individual has a unique constitution (Prakriti) — a combination of Vata, Pitta, and Kapha doshas — and tailors treatment, diet, and lifestyle accordingly.

Yoga sessions at Samarpan focus on Hatha Yoga asanas, breathwork (Pranayama), and body awareness — healing the physical and pranic body. Meditation sessions, including Ajapa Japa, work at a subtler level — training attention, dissolving mental patterns, and cultivating inner stillness.
