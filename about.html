Our Mission

Why Samarpan Exists

Modern healthcare has achieved extraordinary progress, yet millions continue to struggle with lifestyle-related diseases, mental stress, and fragmented care.

At the same time, centuries of traditional knowledge remain underexplored in the light of contemporary science.

Samarpan was created to bring these worlds together — not by replacing one with the other, but by encouraging thoughtful dialogue, research, and responsible integration.

Our Vision

A World Where Every Human Being
Knows Their Own Nature.

We see Samarpan growing into a formal research institution — a place where clinical care, education, and evidence-based inquiry stand on equal footing with tradition, so that healing is never a matter of chance, and never a matter of geography.

What Makes Us Different

01

Dialogue, Not Replacement

We don't ask tradition to prove itself to science, or science to defer to tradition. We hold both in respectful, evidence-seeking conversation.

02

Prevention, Not Just Treatment

Our work begins long before illness — in awareness, daily practice, and lifestyle, so that treatment is rarely the first resort.

03

Research-Institution Mindset

Every protocol, article, and session is built to be documented and studied — not just delivered. We are building toward formal research, not just service.

04

A Living Community

Doctors, researchers, students, and volunteers learn and build alongside the people we serve — nobody is only a recipient of care.

Founder & Lead Practitioner

Harsh Ranjan

Yoga · Ayurveda · Psychology · Mantra

Harsh Ranjan is the founder of Samarpan and an integrative wellness practitioner with over six years of dedicated immersion in the Vedic sciences. His journey began in the ashrams of India — living, breathing, and absorbing the teachings of yoga, Ayurveda, and meditation not as subjects of study, but as a way of life.

Trained in classical Hatha Yoga, Pranayama, Yoga Nidra, Ajapa Japa, Ayurvedic constitution analysis, and depth psychology, Harsh brings a rare integration of inner science with practical healing tools. His sessions are characterised by deep listening, precision, and an ability to meet each person exactly where they are.

Today, he guides clients from 9+ countries — online and in sacred retreat spaces across India — offering a bridge between ancient wisdom and the modern human experience.

Lella Naga
Durga Bhavani

Ayurveda · BAMS · Sareer Kriya Gold Medallist

Dr. Lella Naga Durga Bhavani is a Founding Member & BAMS (Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery) doctor from Dr. Noori Ramasastry Ayurvedic Medical College, Andhra Pradesh — and a Gold Medallist in Sareer Kriya (Ayurvedic Physiology), the most foundational science of the human body in classical Ayurveda.

Her academic distinction reflects not just intellectual mastery, but a deep and sincere devotion to the classical texts — the Charaka Samhita, Sushruta Samhita, and Ashtanga Hridayam — which she approaches with the reverence of a true student of the tradition.

At Samarpan, Leela contributes her rigorous classical Ayurvedic knowledge through the Wisdom Stream Journal, community outreach, and research collaboration — bridging the living tradition of the Vaidyas with the modern wellness seeker.

Dr. Padigireddy Bhuvaneswari

Ayurvedic Physician · Pranic Healer · Sustainable Living Advocate

Dr. Padigireddy Bhuvaneswari is a BAMS, Ayurvedacharya from Sri Sri College of Ayurvedic Science and Research, Bengaluru, who secured distinctions across five core subjects and completed her clinical internship in January 2025. She practices from Nellore, Andhra Pradesh, marrying ancestral scriptural purity with modern integrative diagnostics.

Her clinical philosophy rejects generic, mass-produced solutions — favouring physician-made botanical medications and scriptural dietary regimens (Ahara Vidhi) tailored to each patient's Prakriti. She is also an advanced Pranic Healer and a dedicated promoter of natural farming and sustainable living.

At Samarpan, she brings this rare combination of classical rigour and ecological consciousness — extending healing to people and the earth alike, guided by the principle of Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam, the world as one family.
