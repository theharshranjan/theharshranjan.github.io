Food-First Approach
Ahara · Food as Medicine

The Plate Before the Prescription

Long before herbs or supplements, classical Ayurveda treats daily food — Ahara — as the primary intervention. What you eat, how much, at what time and in what combination is considered to shape tissue, immunity, energy and mind more directly than almost anything else. Nutrition planning here begins by correcting the plate, not replacing it.

What You Eat

Quality and combination of ingredients — fresh, seasonal, suited to your constitution rather than a fixed food list.

When You Eat

Meal timing aligned to your digestive rhythm across the day, so Agni is working with you rather than against you.

How You Eat

Attention and pace at the table — considered as influential on digestion as the food itself.

Shad Rasa · The Six Tastes

Every Meal, All Six Tastes

Ayurveda reads every ingredient through taste rather than calories or macros. A meal that includes sweet, sour, salty, pungent, bitter and astringent notes — in proportions suited to you — is considered naturally satisfying and easier to digest completely.

Sweet
Madhura
Nourishes tissue, calms and grounds — grains, milk, root vegetables.
Sour
Amla
Stimulates Agni and appetite — citrus, yogurt, fermented food.
Salty
Lavana
Aids digestion and hydration — rock salt, sea vegetables, broths.
Pungent
Katu
Clears and warms — ginger, chili, black pepper, mustard.
Bitter
Tikta
Detoxifies and lightens — leafy greens, turmeric, fenugreek.
Astringent
Kashaya
Tightens and tones tissue — lentils, pomegranate, unripe banana.
Tridosha · Constitution-Based Eating

Your Plate Follows Your Dosha

The same food doesn't suit every body. Recommendations shift depending on which dosha — Vata, Pitta or Kapha — tends to run high in you, especially where weight and digestion are concerned.

Tendency

Irregular appetite, gas and bloating, dry skin, variable energy and digestion.

Favour

  • Warm, cooked, moist meals
  • Ghee and healthy fats
  • Sweet, sour, salty tastes
  • Regular meal timing

Reduce

  • Cold, raw, dry foods
  • Excess bitter & astringent
  • Skipping meals
  • Eating on the go

Tendency

Strong appetite, acidity, heat intolerance, tendency toward inflammation when unbalanced.

Favour

  • Cooling, fresh foods
  • Sweet, bitter, astringent tastes
  • Leafy greens, cucumber, coconut
  • Moderate portions

Reduce

  • Fried & excessively spicy food
  • Excess sour & salty tastes
  • Alcohol, caffeine in excess
  • Eating when angry or rushed

Tendency

Slow digestion, weight gain, sluggishness, comfort in routine but resistance to change.

Favour

  • Light, warm, dry-ish meals
  • Pungent, bitter, astringent tastes
  • Legumes, steamed vegetables
  • Largest meal at midday

Reduce

  • Heavy, oily, sweet foods
  • Dairy in excess
  • Late, large dinners
  • Snacking between meals
Agni · The Digestive Fire

A Day Built Around Your Fire

Ayurveda treats Agni — digestive fire — as the deciding factor between food that becomes energy and food that becomes excess weight or toxin (Ama). Timing meals to its natural rhythm across the day is one of the simplest, highest-leverage changes in a diet plan.

Early Morning

Wake the Fire Gently

Warm water, sometimes with lemon, before anything else. Agni is just waking — a light start prepares it without overwhelming it.

Midday

Eat Your Largest Meal

Digestive fire peaks around noon. This is the meal that can carry the most protein, grain and fat with the least residue.

Afternoon

Let It Work

Avoid heavy snacking so Agni can fully process the midday meal before it's asked to start again.

Evening

Eat Light, Eat Early

Agni naturally softens by evening. A lighter, earlier dinner is considered easier to digest and gentler on sleep and weight.

Where Plans Usually Start

Weight & Digestion, Addressed Together

These are the two concerns nearly every nutrition consultation begins with — and in Ayurveda, they're treated as closely linked rather than separate problems.

Weight

Sustainable, Not Restrictive

Rather than calorie-cutting, weight plans here work by correcting Agni, taste balance and meal timing — so the body stops storing what it can't fully process.

  • Portion & timing correction over elimination diets
  • Reducing heavy, oily and late-night eating
  • Building meals around your dominant dosha
Digestion

Fixing the Root, Not the Symptom

Bloating, acidity and irregularity are read as signs of Agni imbalance first — addressed through food combination, spice use and timing before anything else.

  • Food-combining guidance for your constitution
  • Digestive spices matched to your dosha
  • A realistic meal rhythm you can keep on workdays

Nutrition guidance here is educational and complementary, not a substitute for medical treatment. Please consult your physician for diagnosed conditions, medication interactions, or before making major dietary changes.
