About Yoga

A Practice for Body, Breath and Mind

Yoga — "to unite" — is a practice over five thousand years old, rooted in the idea that wellbeing arises from aligning body, breath and mind. Rather than treating movement as exercise alone, it looks at each person's unique constitution and goals to guide posture, breathwork and meditation.

Guided Practice · Asana Alignment

Asana

Physical postures that build strength, flexibility and steadiness in the body.

Pranayama

Breath-control techniques that regulate the nervous system and calm the mind.

Dhyana

Meditation — sustained, focused awareness cultivated through consistent practice.

Bandha

Internal energy locks that stabilise posture and direct the flow of prana.

Mudra

Hand and body gestures believed to channel and seal energy during practice.

Drishti

A fixed gaze point used to steady attention and deepen concentration in asana.

Ashtanga

The eight classical limbs of yoga — from ethics to posture to absorption.

Prana

Vital life-force energy, cultivated and balanced through breath and movement.

Find Your Practice

Three Ways to Practice

Every style falls somewhere on a spectrum from calming to energizing. Understanding where you need to be is the starting point for a personalised practice plan.

Calming · Balancing · Energizing

Calming

Yin · Restorative · Yoga Nidra

Slow, supported practices that soothe an overactive nervous system. Best for stress, anxiety, insomnia and burnout.

Balancing

Hatha · Iyengar · Pranayama

Steady, alignment-focused practices that build strength without overheating the system. Best for joint health, focus and steady energy.

Energizing

Vinyasa · Ashtanga · Surya Namaskar

Dynamic, heat-building sequences that shake off stagnation. Best for sluggishness, weight goals and low motivation.

Practised Mindfully, Not a Replacement

Yoga can complement conventional care, but it is not a substitute for it. Please continue any prescribed medical treatment and consult your physician before starting a new practice — especially during pregnancy, with injuries, for children, or alongside existing medication.
